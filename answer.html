CYBERSECURITY RISKS OF AR

Understanding the Risks

Augmented Reality (AR) technologies, particularly those involving facial filters, pose significant cybersecurity risks. While these applications enhance user experience, they also collect sensitive biometric data that can be exploited.

What Data is Collected?

AR applications often gather detailed facial measurements and features, which constitute sensitive biometric data. Users may not be fully aware of the extent of data being collected, leading to potential misuse.

  • Facial Recognition Data: This data can be exploited for identity theft or unauthorised access.
  • Manipulation Risks: Fake AR content can be injected to manipulate user perception and behavior.
  • Unauthorised Access: Vulnerabilities in AR applications could allow unauthorised access to camera feeds or other sensitive data.

Why Should You Care?

As AR technologies become more integrated into our daily lives, understanding the implications for privacy and security is crucial. Users must critically assess the agreements they enter into when using AR filters and be aware of the potential risks to their digital lives.
